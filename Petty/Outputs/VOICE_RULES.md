# Voice Rules — Petty

## Purpose
Define tone and language rules for all product copy, emails, and marketing.

## Tone
Direct. Warm. Confident. Never cutesy. Petty is a tool used by someone who is worried about their animal — respect that.

## Triggers
- All user-facing UI copy
- Onboarding flows
- Error messages
- Email notifications
- Marketing site
- Generated summary headings and section titles

## Dos
- Use active voice
- Short, clear sentences
- Talk to the user directly ("you", "your")
- Plain language — no jargon unless the user's vet uses it
- Lead with the action or outcome
- Acknowledge the pet's name when we know it ("Add an entry for {{pet.name}}")

## Don'ts
- No passive voice ("has been sent" → "we sent")
- No corporate filler ("leverage", "seamless", "synergy", "empower", "robust")
- Don't over-apologise in error states — one short sentence is enough
- No ellipsis in UI copy
- No cute pet-parent language ("fur-baby", "pup-dates", "paw-fect")
- No medical claims — we don't diagnose, we organise

## Examples

### Empty States
- Good: "No pet yet. Add your first one."
- Good: "No entries for Luna yet. Log what you noticed today."
- Bad: "It seems you haven't created anything at this time."

### Error Messages
- Good: "Something went wrong. Try again."
- Good: "That file is over 100 MB. Try a smaller one."
- Bad: "An unexpected error has occurred."

### CTAs
- Good: "Add pet"
- Good: "Generate summary"
- Good: "Download PDF"
- Bad: "Click here to begin"

### Onboarding
- Good: "Tell us about your pet. You can change any of this later."
- Good: "Log anything you noticed — a symptom, a meal, a photo."
- Bad: "Let's embark on a journey together."

### Summary Section Titles
- Good: "What I noticed", "When it happened", "What I did about it", "Current meds"
- Bad: "Observational Data", "Pet Wellness Narrative"

### Email
- Good subject: "Your Luna summary is ready"
- Bad subject: "Your pet health wellness report is now available for download"

## Inclusivity
- Don't assume pronouns for the pet or the owner
- Don't assume one owner — other caretakers may be in the household
- Don't assume a vet relationship — some users are still shopping for one
